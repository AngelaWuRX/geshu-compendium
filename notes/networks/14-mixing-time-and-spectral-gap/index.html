    Mixing Time, Conductance, Spectral Gap¶

    Setting: RW on connected undirected \(G=(V,E)\), \(P=D^{-1}A\), stationary \(\pi_i=d_i/(2|E|)\).

    Definitions¶

    • \(\mu_{t,x}(v) = (P^t)_{xv}\), \(\bar d(t)=\max_{x,y}\|\mu_{t,x}-\mu_{t,y}\|_{TV}\), \(d(t)=\max_x\|\mu_{t,x}-\pi\|_{TV}\).
    • Mixing time: \(\tau = \min\{t : \bar d(t)\leq 1/e\}\).
    • Symmetrized matrix: \(\tilde P = D^{1/2}PD^{-1/2} = D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}\) (symmetric, same eigenvalues as \(P\)).
    • Eigenvalues of \(P\): \(1=\lambda_1\geq \lambda_2\geq\cdots\geq\lambda_n\geq -1\).
    • Spectral gap: \(1-\lambda_2\).
    • Normalized Laplacian: \(\tilde L = I - \tilde P = D^{-1/2}(D-A)D^{-1/2}\), eigenvalues \(\Lambda_i = 1-\lambda_i\in[0,2]\).
    • Flow: \(Q_{ij}=\pi_i P_{ij}=A_{ij}/(2|E|)\).
    • Conductance: \(\phi(S) = \frac{\sum_{i\in S, j\in S^c}Q_{ij}}{\pi(S)\pi(S^c)} = \frac{\text{cut}(S,S^c)\,\text{vol}(V)}{\text{vol}(S)\,\text{vol}(S^c)}\), \(\phi=\min_{\emptyset\neq S\subsetneq V}\phi(S)\).
    • Expansion: \(\alpha(S) = \frac{\text{cut}(S,S^c)}{\min(|S|,|S^c|)}\), \(\alpha=\min_S\alpha(S)\).

    Lemma 1 (Submultiplicativity of \(\bar d\))¶

    \(\bar d(s+t) \leq \bar d(s)\bar d(t)\), hence \(\bar d(k\tau)\leq e^{-k}\). Also \(d(t)\leq \bar d(t)\leq 2d(t)\).

    (Standard coupling argument; HW.)

    Lemma 2 (Eigenvalues of \(P\) are real, in \([-1,1]\))¶

    Proof. \(\tilde P=D^{-1/2}AD^{-1/2}\) symmetric ⇒ real eigenvalues. \(P=D^{-1/2}\tilde P D^{1/2}\) similar to \(\tilde P\) ⇒ same eigenvalues. Bound \(|\lambda|\leq 1\) as in the MC lemma.

    Lemma 3 (First eigenvector of \(\tilde P\))¶

    \(\varphi_1(v)=\sqrt{\pi(v)}\) satisfies \(\tilde P\varphi_1=\varphi_1\), \(\|\varphi_1\|_2=1\).

    Proof. \((\tilde P\varphi_1)_x = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{d_x}}\sum_y A_{xy}\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{d_y}}\sqrt{d_y/(2|E|)} = \sqrt{d_x/(2|E|)}=\varphi_1(x)\). Norm: \(\sum\pi(x)=1\).

    Lemma 4 (Quadratic form of Laplacian)¶

    For \(L=D-A\): \(f^T L f = \tfrac12\sum_{i,j}(f_i-f_j)^2 A_{ij}\).

    Proof. RHS \(=\sum_i f_i^2 d_i - \sum_{ij}f_i A_{ij}f_j = f^T(D-A)f\).

    Lemma 5 (Expansion ↔ Conductance)¶

    \[\frac{\alpha}{d_{\max}} \leq \phi \leq 2\frac{\alpha}{d_{\min}}.\]

    Proof. \(\pi(S)=\sum_{x\in S}d_x/(2|E|)\), with bounds \(\frac{d_{\min}|S|}{2|E|}\leq \pi(S)\leq \frac{d_{\max}|S|}{2|E|}\), and \(\min\{\pi(S),\pi(S^c)\} \geq \pi(S)\pi(S^c) \geq \tfrac12\min\{\pi(S),\pi(S^c)\}\). Plug into \(\phi(S)\).

    Theorem 1 (Cheeger inequality)¶

    \[\frac{\phi^2}{8} \leq 1-\lambda_2 \leq \phi.\]

    Step A — Variational form of \(1-\lambda_2\)¶

    $\(1-\lambda_2 = \min_{\varphi\perp\varphi_1}\frac{\varphi^T(I-\tilde P)\varphi}{\varphi^T\varphi}.\)$ This is just Rayleigh on the symmetric \(\tilde L\), with first eigenvector \(\varphi_1\).

    Step B — Test vector for \(\phi(S)\)¶

    Define \(f_S(i) = \big[\pi(S^c)\mathbf 1_{i\in S} - \pi(S)\mathbf 1_{i\in S^c}\big]\sqrt{d_i}\).

    Then (i) \(f_S \perp \varphi_1\), (ii) \(\phi(S) = \dfrac{f_S^T(I-\tilde P)f_S}{f_S^T f_S}\).

    Proof of (i). \(\sum_x f_S(x)\varphi_1(x)=\tfrac{1}{\sqrt{2|E|}}\sum_x d_x[\pi(S^c)\mathbf 1_S - \pi(S)\mathbf 1_{S^c}] = \sqrt{2|E|}(\pi(S)\pi(S^c)-\pi(S^c)\pi(S)) = 0\).

    Proof of (ii). Let \(\tilde f_i=f_i/\sqrt{d_i}\) so \(\tilde f\) is the two-level \(\pi(S^c)\mathbf 1_S-\pi(S)\mathbf 1_{S^c}\). Then - Numerator: \(f^T(I-\tilde P)f = \tilde f^T(D-A)\tilde f = \tfrac12\sum A_{ij}(\tilde f_i-\tilde f_j)^2\). Only cross-edges contribute, \(|\tilde f_i-\tilde f_j|=\pi(S)+\pi(S^c)=1\) ⇒ \(=\sum_{i\in S, j\in S^c}A_{ij}\). - Denominator: \(f^Tf = \sum_i d_i[\pi(S^c)^2\mathbf 1_S+\pi(S)^2\mathbf 1_{S^c}] = 2|E|[\pi(S)\pi(S^c)^2+\pi(S^c)\pi(S)^2] = 2|E|\pi(S)\pi(S^c)\). - Ratio: \(\frac{\text{cut}(S,S^c)}{2|E|\pi(S)\pi(S^c)} = \phi(S)\).

    Step C — Upper bound \(1-\lambda_2 \leq \phi\)¶

    By (i)+(ii) and Step A: \(1-\lambda_2 \leq \phi(S)\) for every \(S\). Take \(\min\) over \(S\).

    Step D — Lower bound \(\phi^2/8 \leq 1-\lambda_2\)¶

    (Harder; not detailed in lecture but stated.) Construct from second eigenvector a "sweep cut" \(S\) with \(\phi(S)^2 \leq 8(1-\lambda_2)\).

    Theorem 2 (Mixing time bound from \(\lambda_2\), lazy chain)¶

    For lazy chain \(M=\tfrac12(I+P)\) with eigenvalues \(\tilde\lambda_i=\tfrac12(\lambda_i+1)\in[0,1]\): $\(\tilde\lambda_2^t \leq \bar d(t) \leq \frac{\tilde\lambda_2^t}{\sqrt{\pi_{\min}}},\quad \pi_{\min}=\min_x\pi(x).\)$

    Proof of upper bound. Spectral decomp: \(\tilde M=\sum_i\tilde\lambda_i\varphi_i\varphi_i^T\), so \(M^t = D^{-1/2}\tilde M^t D^{1/2}\) gives $\(\mu_{t,x}(v) = M^t_{xv} = \pi(v) + \sum_{i\geq 2}\tilde\lambda_i^t\varphi_i(x)\varphi_i(v)\sqrt{d_v/d_x}\)$ (the \(i=1\) term equals \(\pi(v)\), since \(\varphi_1(x)\varphi_1(v)\sqrt{d_v/d_x}=\sqrt{d_x d_v}/(2|E|)\sqrt{d_v/d_x}=d_v/(2|E|)=\pi(v)\)).

    Then $\(\|\mu_{t,x}-\pi\|_1 \leq \tilde\lambda_2^t \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{d_{\min}}}\sum_i|\varphi_i(x)|\sum_v|\varphi_i(v)|\sqrt{d_v}.\)$ By Cauchy-Schwarz with \(\sum_i\varphi_i(x)^2=1\) (since \(\varphi_i\) form an ONB of \(\mathbb R^n\) ⇒ \(\sum_i\varphi_i\varphi_i^T=I\), diagonal entry =1) and \(\sum_i\big(\sum_v\varphi_i(v)\sqrt{d_v}\big)^2 = \sum_v d_v = 2|E|\): $\(\|\mu_{t,x}-\pi\|_1 \leq \tilde\lambda_2^t\sqrt{2|E|/d_{\min}} = \tilde\lambda_2^t/\sqrt{\pi_{\min}}.\)$ Since \(\bar d(t)=\max\tfrac12\|\mu_{t,x}-\mu_{t,y}\|_1\leq\max\|\mu_{t,x}-\pi\|_1\), done.

    Common pitfalls¶

    • Confusing \(\lambda_n\) (\(-1\) end, periodicity) with \(\lambda_2\) (mixing). The lazy chain pushes everything to \([0,1]\) so only \(\lambda_2\) matters.
    • \(\varphi_1\) is the eigenvector of \(\tilde P\), not of \(P\). The eigenvector of \(P\) for \(\lambda=1\) is \(\mathbf 1\).
    • Test vector \(f_S\) has factor \(\sqrt{d_i}\) (so it lives in the \(\tilde P\) space).
